"Conceptualization involves the process of developing and refining ideas, often through a series of mental detours, and turning them into tangible plans or solutions. This process is characterized by a willingness to explore and understand complex concepts, such as the nature of evil, the ethics of promoting certain products, or the consequences of certain actions. It also involves considering multiple perspectives, including those of scientists, doctors, and everyday people, and weighing the pros and cons of different approaches. Ultimately, conceptualization is about finding creative solutions to real-world problems and making informed decisions that take into account various factors and potential outcomes."
