"The poetic style in Kushner's treatment of dialogue serves to convey the emotional depth and complexity of the characters, often blurring the lines between ordinary conversation and poetic expression. This style is used to explore themes of identity, morality, and the human condition, and is often employed to create a sense of intimacy and immediacy with the reader. In contrast, ordinary prosaic dialogue is used to convey more straightforward information and to advance the plot. The poetic style is often used to create a sense of tension, to explore the inner lives of the characters, and to convey the emotional resonance of a particular moment or scene."
